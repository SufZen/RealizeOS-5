# Method: Competitive Analysis

## Purpose
Systematically evaluate competitors to find strategic opportunities.

## When to Use
When entering a new market, launching a new offering, or reviewing positioning.

## Steps

1. **Identify competitors** — Direct (same offering), indirect (different approach, same problem), and adjacent (could expand into your space)
2. **Map their positioning:**
   | Competitor | Target | Price Point | Key UVP | Weakness |
   |-----------|--------|-------------|---------|----------|
   | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
3. **Analyze their content** — What topics do they cover? What voice do they use? Where do they publish?
4. **Find the gaps** — Where is nobody competing well? What needs do they leave unmet?
5. **Assess your advantage** — Where do you have a genuine edge? (Experience, tech, price, speed, depth)
6. **Formulate positioning** — Complete: "For [audience] who [need], we are the [category] that [differentiator] unlike [competition] because [reason]."

## Expected Output
A positioning matrix with competitive gaps and your strategic advantage.

## Notes
- Focus on customer pain, not feature comparison
- Competitor analysis should inform strategy, not dictate it
- Update quarterly — markets shift fast

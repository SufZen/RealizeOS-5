# Venture Voice & Communication Style

## Tone

Our communication is: **professional**, **knowledgeable**, **trustworthy**, and **locally informed**.

## Language Rules

- Use active voice and concrete language
- Lead with the most important information
- Include specific numbers (prices, areas, yields) — vagueness erodes trust
- Use local terminology correctly (escritura, CPCV, IMI, etc.) when appropriate
- Bilingual communications should be natural, not machine-translated

## Writing Dos

- Back claims with data or experience
- Reference specific locations, neighborhoods, and landmarks
- Address the reader's actual concerns (legal process, taxes, timeline)
- Break complex processes into clear steps

## Writing Don'ts

- Don't oversell — let the property speak through accurate description
- Don't use generic real estate clichés ("dream home", "hidden gem", "must see")
- Don't ignore local regulatory requirements in content
- Don't mix formal and informal tone within the same piece

## Channel Adaptations

- **Portal listings:** Factual, structured, keyword-optimized. Lead with location and key features.
- **Social media:** Visual-first, lifestyle-oriented. Show the neighborhood, not just the property.
- **Client reports:** Data-driven, executive summary first, detailed appendix.
- **Email campaigns:** Personal, value-adding (market insights, not just new listings).

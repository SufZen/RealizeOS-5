# Buying Property in Portugal — Process Guide

## Overview

The Portuguese property buying process typically takes 4-12 weeks from offer acceptance to completion, depending on mortgage requirements and document readiness.

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Preparation
- Obtain NIF (Número de Identificação Fiscal) — required for all transactions
- Open a Portuguese bank account (recommended but not mandatory)
- Engage a lawyer (advogado) — strongly recommended for foreigners
- Obtain mortgage pre-approval if financing (typically 60-80% LTV for non-residents)

### 2. Property Search & Due Diligence
- Verify property documentation:
  - **Caderneta Predial** (tax authority property record)
  - **Certidão Permanente** (land registry certificate)
  - **Licença de Utilização** (usage license from Câmara Municipal)
  - **Ficha Técnica de Habitação** (technical specifications, post-2004 buildings)
  - **Certificado Energético** (energy certificate)
- Check for liens, encumbrances, or ongoing legal issues
- Verify property boundaries and registered area match reality

### 3. CPCV (Promissory Contract)
- Legally binding agreement between buyer and seller
- Typical deposit: 10-20% of purchase price
- Sets the completion deadline (usually 60-90 days)
- Penalty for withdrawal: buyer loses deposit; seller pays double deposit back
- Must include: property identification, price, payment terms, deadline, penalty clauses

### 4. Mortgage (if applicable)
- Bank appraisal of property
- Mortgage approval and deed preparation
- Typical terms: 20-30 years, variable or fixed rate

### 5. Escritura (Final Deed)
- Signed at a notary office (Cartório Notarial) or land registry (Conservatória)
- All parties present (or represented by power of attorney)
- Taxes paid: IMT (transfer tax) + Imposto de Selo (stamp duty)
- Balance of purchase price transferred
- Keys handed over

### 6. Post-Completion
- Register property at Conservatória (if not already done at deed signing)
- Update IMI records at Finanças
- Transfer utility contracts (water, electricity, gas)
- Register with condominium (if apartment)

## Costs for Buyer

| Cost | Typical Amount |
|---|---|
| IMT (transfer tax) | 0-8% (progressive, based on price and type) |
| Stamp duty | 0.8% of purchase price |
| Notary fees | EUR 200-500 |
| Registration fees | EUR 200-400 |
| Legal fees | EUR 1,500-3,000+ (varies by complexity) |
| Mortgage costs | ~1% of loan amount (bank fees, appraisal) |

## IMT Rates (Mainland, Non-Primary Residence)

| Property Value | Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to EUR 97,064 | 1% |
| EUR 97,064 — 132,774 | 2% |
| EUR 132,774 — 181,034 | 5% |
| EUR 181,034 — 301,688 | 7% |
| EUR 301,688 — 578,598 | 8% |
| Above EUR 578,598 | 7.5% (single rate) |

*Note: Rates and brackets are updated periodically. Always verify current rates.*

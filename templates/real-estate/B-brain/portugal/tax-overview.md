# Portuguese Property Taxes — Overview

## Transaction Taxes (One-Time)

### IMT (Imposto Municipal sobre Transmissões Onerosas de Imóveis)
- Paid before the escritura (deed signing)
- Progressive rates from 0% to 8% based on property value
- Different rates for: primary residence, secondary/investment, rural, commercial
- Paid at Finanças or online via Portal das Finanças

### Stamp Duty (Imposto de Selo)
- 0.8% of purchase price (or tax value, whichever is higher)
- Paid alongside IMT before the escritura
- Also applies to mortgages: 0.6% of loan amount

## Annual Taxes

### IMI (Imposto Municipal sobre Imóveis)
- Annual property tax based on VPT (Valor Patrimonial Tributário)
- Rates set by each municipality:
  - Urban properties: 0.3% to 0.45%
  - Rural properties: 0.8%
- Paid in 1-3 installments (April, July, November)
- Properties owned by entities in blacklisted jurisdictions: 7.5%

### AIMI (Adicional ao IMI)
- Wealth tax on total property portfolio value
- Applies to individuals with property VPT above EUR 600,000 (or EUR 1,200,000 for couples)
- Rates: 0.7% (individuals), 0.4% (companies), 1% (high-value)

## Rental Income Tax

### Residents
- Taxed as part of IRS (income tax) at progressive rates (14.5% to 48%)
- Special flat rate options for certain rental types

### Non-Residents
- Flat 25% withholding tax on gross rental income
- Can opt into the NHR (Non-Habitual Resident) regime for potential benefits

### Long-Term Rental Incentives
- Contracts 5-10 years: reduced tax rates available
- Affordable housing programs: further reductions

## Capital Gains Tax (at Sale)

### Residents
- 50% of the gain is included in taxable income
- Exempt if reinvested in primary residence (within 36 months)
- Acquisition cost adjusted for inflation (coeficientes de desvalorização monetária)
- Deductible expenses: renovation costs (within 12 years), sales costs

### Non-Residents
- Flat 28% on the total gain (no 50% reduction)
- Deductible costs same as residents
- Possible treaty benefits depending on country of residence

*Note: Tax laws change. Always verify current rates and consult a qualified tax advisor.*

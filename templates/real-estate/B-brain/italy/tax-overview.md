# Italian Property Taxes — Overview

## Transaction Taxes (One-Time)

### Buying from Private Seller (Resale)
| Tax | Primary Residence | Secondary/Investment |
|---|---|---|
| Imposta di Registro | 2% of cadastral value | 9% of cadastral value |
| Imposta Ipotecaria | EUR 50 | EUR 50 |
| Imposta Catastale | EUR 50 | EUR 50 |

### Buying from Developer (New Build)
| Tax | Primary Residence | Secondary/Investment |
|---|---|---|
| IVA | 4% of sale price | 10% of sale price (22% for luxury) |
| Imposta di Registro | EUR 200 | EUR 200 |
| Imposta Ipotecaria | EUR 200 | EUR 200 |
| Imposta Catastale | EUR 200 | EUR 200 |

*Note: Cadastral value is typically 50-70% lower than market value for resale properties.*

## Annual Taxes

### IMU (Imposta Municipale Unica)
- Annual property tax on ALL properties except primary residence (abitazione principale)
- Rate: 0.76% standard (municipalities can adjust 0.46%-1.06%)
- Based on rendita catastale, multiplied by coefficient (160 for residential)
- Paid in two installments: June and December

### TARI (Tassa sui Rifiuti)
- Waste collection tax
- Based on property size (m²) and occupants
- Varies significantly by municipality

## Rental Income Tax

### Cedolare Secca (Flat Tax Option)
- 21% flat tax on gross rental income (long-term contracts)
- 10% for "canone concordato" (agreed rent) contracts in high-demand municipalities
- Replaces: IRPEF, registration tax, and stamp duty on contract
- Must be elected at contract registration

### Standard Taxation
- Rental income added to IRPEF (progressive rates 23%-43%)
- 5% deduction for expenses (forfait)
- Contract registration: 2% of annual rent

## Capital Gains Tax

- Taxable if sold within 5 years of purchase
- After 5 years: exempt
- Rate: 26% flat tax or included in IRPEF (taxpayer's choice)
- Primary residence: always exempt

*Note: Tax laws change frequently. Consult a commercialista for current advice.*

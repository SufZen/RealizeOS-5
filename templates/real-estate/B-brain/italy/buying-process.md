# Buying Property in Italy — Process Guide

## Overview

The Italian property buying process typically takes 8-16 weeks from offer acceptance. The process is notary-centric — the notaio plays a central role in verifying legality and executing the transfer.

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Preparation
- Obtain Codice Fiscale (tax code) — required for all transactions
- Open an Italian bank account (strongly recommended)
- Engage a lawyer (avvocato) and/or geometra for due diligence
- Obtain mortgage pre-approval if financing

### 2. Proposta di Acquisto (Purchase Offer)
- Written offer with deposit (caparra confirmatoria) — typically EUR 5,000-10,000
- Binding on buyer once accepted by seller
- Usually valid for 15-30 days

### 3. Compromesso (Preliminary Contract)
- Detailed binding agreement between buyer and seller
- Deposit: typically 10-30% of purchase price
- Sets the completion deadline (usually 60-90 days)
- Registered with Agenzia delle Entrate (registro fisso: EUR 200 + 0.5% of deposit)

### 4. Due Diligence
- **Visura Catastale** — cadastral data (size, category, rendita)
- **Visura Ipotecaria** — check for mortgages, liens, seizures
- **Conformità Urbanistica** — verify building permits and legality
- **Conformità Catastale** — verify floor plan matches catasto
- **APE** (Attestato di Prestazione Energetica) — energy certificate
- Check condominium status and pending expenses

### 5. Rogito (Notarial Deed)
- Final transfer deed signed before a notaio (public official)
- Notaio verifies: ownership, no liens, urban/cadastral conformity, tax compliance
- Balance paid (typically via bank draft — assegno circolare)
- Taxes paid at signing
- Keys handed over

### 6. Post-Completion
- Notaio registers the deed at Conservatoria dei Registri Immobiliari
- Update catasto records
- Transfer utility contracts (voltura)
- TARI registration (waste tax)

## Costs for Buyer

| Cost | Amount |
|---|---|
| Imposta di Registro | 2% (primary) or 9% (secondary) of cadastral value |
| IVA | 4% (primary) or 10% (secondary) — only from developer |
| Cadastral tax | EUR 50 (from developer) or 1% (resale) |
| Mortgage tax | EUR 50 (from developer) or 2% (resale) |
| Notary fees | EUR 2,000-5,000+ |
| Agency commission | 2-4% + IVA (22%) |
| Legal fees | EUR 1,500-3,000 |

*Note: "Primary residence" (prima casa) benefits require residency within 18 months. Tax rates change — verify current rates.*

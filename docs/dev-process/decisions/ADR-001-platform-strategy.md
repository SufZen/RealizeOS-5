# ADR-001: Platform Strategy — Ship Intelligence Engine, Not Feature Clones

> Status: **Accepted**
> Date: 2026-03-10
> Context: Defining RealizeOS competitive positioning against OpenClaw

## Decision

Position RealizeOS as an **intelligence-depth platform** rather than a channel-breadth competitor. Ship the core intelligence engine (7 pillars) and extensibility layers (Tool SDK, Workflow Engine, Channel Adapters) — let users build their own systems on top.

## Why

### For Platform Strategy
- OpenClaw has 24+ channels and native apps — competing on channel count is a losing game
- Our unique strengths (multi-entity identity, 7-layer prompts, creative pipelines, self-evolution) are deep intelligence features no competitor has
- Target market (entrepreneurs managing multiple brands) needs intelligence, not more chat interfaces
- Platform approach creates ecosystem effects (skills, tools, adapters contributed by community)

### Against Feature-Cloning
- Copying OpenClaw's channel list produces a worse version of OpenClaw
- Our team size cannot maintain 24 channel adapters
- Intelligence depth is defensible; channel breadth is not

## Consequences

- We need excellent extensibility (Tool SDK, Channel Adapters) so users add their own integrations
- We ship fewer built-in channels (Telegram, WhatsApp, Web) but each one polished
- Marketing must communicate intelligence value, not feature list
- Community engagement becomes important (skill marketplace, adapter contributions)
